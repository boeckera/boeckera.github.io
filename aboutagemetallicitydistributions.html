Recovery of age-metallicity distributions from integrated spectra

In this work I analyzed the stellar populations of M54, the nucleus of the Sagittarius Dwarf Galaxy, as recovered from its integrated spectrum coming from observations with MUSE and WiFes. Due to the proximity of M54, its individual stars can be resolved and characterized by their ages and metallicities (as was done by Alfaro-Cuello et al., 2019). Therefore, M54 provides an ideal test case of comparing age-metallicity distributions recovered from individual stellar measurements (panel a) with those recovered from the integrated spectrum (panel b). As seen with both methods, M54 consists of old, metal-poor stars as well as younger, metal-rich stars.

In my paper, I also show, among other results, that the integrated light measurements are robust against blue horizontal branch stars as well as largely insensitive to the magnitude limit of the observations. This study provides strong evidence that age-metallicity distrubtions can be reliably detected from integrated spectra, which will provide a whole new wealth of information for many extragalactic systems, where previously only average stellar population quantities were available.
