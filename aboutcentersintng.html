The stellar assembly of galactic centers in TNG50

At the moment, I am studying the center of TNG50 simulated galaxies. The resolution as well as simulated volumne of TNG50 makes it possible to resolve the central few hundred parsecs for thousands of galaxies, each with their own unique formation history. I trace stellar particles back in time and determine whether they belong to an "in-situ" (born in the center), "migrated" (migrated to the center) or "ex-situ" (brought in by mergers) origin. For this particular galaxy above, 45% of all the stars in the center are of in-situ, 15% of migrated and 40% of ex-situ origin. There are two high mass mergers (yellow) that happened to this galaxy approximately 3 and 7 Gyr ago, that brought in the major of the ex-situ stars in the center and also triggered a lot of central star formation. This work is still in progress so more results will follow here soon!
